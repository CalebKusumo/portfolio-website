Parameter Reference Guide
Wheelbase (L)
Distance between front and rear axle centrelines. Longer wheelbase increases stability and reduces sensitivity, but increases minimum turn radius.
Ackermann Condition
The Ackermann condition ensures both wheels point toward a common turn centre. cot δₒ − cot δᵢ = w / (l · x) where x = Ackermann % / 100. At 100%, the inner wheel traces a tighter circle than the outer.
Steer Camber Formula
Induced camber when the wheel is steered through angle θ:
C = KPI × (1 − cos θ) − Caster × sin θ + SC
KPI creates positive camber gain; caster subtracts camber on the inner wheel. SC is the static (setup) camber.
Kingpin Inclination (KPI)
Inward tilt of the steering axis as viewed from the front. Reduces scrub radius and creates a jacking effect that returns the wheel to centre. Typical FSAE: 8–15°.
Caster Angle
Rearward tilt of the steering axis as viewed from the side. Positive caster increases mechanical trail and self-aligning torque, giving steering feel. Typical FSAE: 3–8°.
U-Joint Velocity Ratio
A single U-joint produces a non-constant output velocity. The ratio fluctuates sinusoidally with shaft rotation θ. Use two U-joints phased 90° apart to cancel the fluctuation and achieve constant velocity output. Operating limit: 32°; recommended max: 20°.
Column Torsion
φ = TL / GJ — angle of twist (target: < 0.01°)
τ = Tr / J — peak shear stress at outer surface
J = π(r_o⁴ − r_i⁴)/2 — polar moment of inertia. Use hollow shafts to maximise J/weight.
Mechanical Trail
Horizontal distance from where the kingpin axis meets the ground to the tyre contact patch. Creates self-aligning torque: T = Fy × trail. More trail = more feedback and effort.
Tie-Rod Height Offset (Bump Steer)
Vertical mismatch between tie-rod inner pivot and wishbone pivot. Zero offset = zero bump steer. Any offset causes the wheel to toe in or out as it travels over bumps. Target < 5 mm for FSAE.
