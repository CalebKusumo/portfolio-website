Parameter Reference Guide
Wheelbase (L)
Distance between front and rear axle centrelines. Longer wheelbase increases stability and reduces sensitivity to driver inputs, but increases the minimum turn radius.
Track Width (T)
Hub-centreline-to-hub-centreline width. Wider track lowers roll, improves lateral grip, and reduces Ackermann angles required. Used to compute inner/outer turn radii.
Rim Inner Diameter
Inner diameter of the rim bead seat. The steer arm must fit within half this value (minus 10 mm safety margin) to avoid contact with the rim during full lock.
Ackermann Percentage
100%: Inner wheel turns more than outer — both tyres trace concentric circles. Perfect for low-speed, low-slip-angle driving.
0%: Both wheels turn equally (parallel steer). Better at high speed where slip angles dominate.
FSAE cars typically run 50–100%.
Steer Arm Length
Lever from the kingpin (steering axis) to the tie-rod ball joint. Longer arm = lower tie-rod force and less rack travel needed, but must fit inside the rim. Key packaging constraint.
Lock-to-Lock Rotation
Total rotation of the steering wheel from full left lock to full right lock. Half this rotation moves the wheel from centre to full δᵢ. Determines steering ratio and pinion radius.
Mechanical Trail
Horizontal distance from where the kingpin axis meets the ground to the tyre contact patch centre. Creates self-aligning torque: T = Fy × trail. More trail = more feedback and effort.
Peak Lateral Force (Fy)
Maximum lateral (cornering) force on the front axle. Use combined left + right tyre capacity. Sets the worst-case aligning torque: T_align = Fy × trail, which drives steering effort.
Tie-Rod Height Offset (Bump Steer)
Vertical mismatch between tie-rod inner pivot and wishbone pivot. Zero offset = zero bump steer. Any offset causes the wheel to toe in or out as it travels over bumps. Target < 5 mm for FSAE.
